Hi! I am a Computer Science graduate from American International University–Bangladesh (AIUB), where I achieved a CGPA of 3.82/4.00. My undergraduate thesis — "Integrating Therapy and Technology: An Exploratory Case Study on the Development of Aids for Dementia" — was advised by Dr. Khandaker Tabin Hasan.

My research sits at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction, tangible and gestural interfaces, and Human-Centered AI, with a focus on designing technology that is expressive, accessible, and deeply human. I am particularly drawn to assistive technologies for aging populations and dementia care, as well as extended reality and embedded interactive systems.

Outside of research, I work as a Product Experience Engineer at The Abroad Company, where I design end-to-end product flows and mentor experiences for an international study advisory platform.

Projects

MoodDial

A tangible, emotion-aware interaction device that lets users express their emotional state via a physical rotary dial instead of a digital interface. Transmits mood data over WiFi to a backend AI model, which delivers supportive, mood-appropriate responses through Telegram, enabling low-effort, asynchronous emotional support.

PromptPad

A tangible AI interaction device that streamlines common AI tasks — such as summarization, translation, and rephrasing — through dedicated physical buttons and real-time visual feedback, reducing interaction friction in everyday AI usage.
